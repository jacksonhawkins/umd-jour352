Mac Miller:

His Life Through the Numbers

Mac Miller was a rapper, producer and songwriter from Pittsburgh before his untimely passing on September 7, 2018. According to the autopsy, Miller died due to an accidental overdose of alcohol, fentanyl and cocaine at the age of 26. Revered by his fans, his music skyrocketed in popularity after his death.

In his 11 year career, Miller released a total of 23 musical projects: 13 mixtapes, six studio albums, two live albums and two extended plays. Beginning his career in 2007 as “Easy Mac,” he started releasing mixtapes as a kid for fun and started picking up popularity due to releasing music videos on a new video sharing platform known as Youtube.

The Sad Reality

Like life, artists die and when they do, their music tends to obtain more attention and streams due to the news of their death. Fans of artists and people who were not familiar tend to latch onto songs from that artist and play it in the coming weeks of the news, bringing up their popularity at the time around death. There have been numerous cases of artists getting more streams post-death announcement such as XXXTentacion, Pop Smoke, JuiceWrld, Prince and even Takeoff from the rap group Migos. Mac Miller is no different besides that his death marked a moment of sustained success and popularity.

The Case of Mac Miller

According to Billboard and Nielsen music, in the week following Miller’s death, his streams grew 970% and his 2018 album “Swimming” made its way to #1 on the Billboard Top 100 albums. In the single month spanning from the end of Aug. 2018 to the end of Sept. 2018, according to Chart Metric, he gained around 6.5 million monthly listeners; this is likely associated with his death announcement.

After his death, his numbers never plateaued or took a nosedive, but rather a steady increase in monthly listeners. Miller increased his total Spotify monthly listeners by 1.76 million from Dec. 2018 to Dec. 2019.

Circles and Mac's 2020 Success

In Jan. 2020, Miller’s estate, along with producer Jon Brion, decided to release his only posthumous album, “Circles”. A week before "Circles” was released, Miller’s estate dropped the first and only single for the album in “Good News.” According to Chart Metric, “Good News” along with the “Circles’' release helped earn Miller nearly 11 million more monthly listeners in the span of one month.
The week he died in 2018, his monthly listenership increased to a little over 14 million listeners. After the Circles release, he continued this trajectory of success by not falling below 13 million listeners to present day.

Mac Miller's Estate and their Tactics

Mac Miller’s estate promised to not release any new albums from Miller as they believe it would be a disservice to what he would want. While this is the case, they have found ways to keep Miller’s name in the spotlight with the approval of some feature verses, playlist placements and in the biggest way; re-releasing some of his off-streaming mixtapes onto streaming services.

Mixtape Re-Releases

The mixtapes his estate released in the span of five years from his death include: “The High Life,” “Macadelic,” “I Love Life, Thank You,” “K.I.D.S” and “Faces.” These re-releases along with playlist placements brought Miller’s monthly listeners to an all-time high, sitting at 24.5 million as of Dec. 2023.

Miller's Life with the Spotlight

Miller was well respected all over the Hip-Hop community for being a positive presence with inspirational words and wisdom. He received a plethora of tribute tweets after his death from Musicians and many Hip-Hop community Icons.

Credit: Dazed

Mac was even somewhat prevalant in popular culture, featuring on songs such as the 2013 Ariana Grande track, “My Way.” He later ended up dating Grande starting in Aug. 2016 to May. 2018, putting him in the spotlight. Even dating and eventually breaking up with Grande did not apply the increase in music popularity that his death announcement did.

The Bottom Line

While people die and the music lasts forever, it often cannot take back the pain that family and fans of Miller felt when he passed away. The music can ease the pain and help mask it, but his estate and fans should rest easy knowing that Miller’s numbers will continue to stay high and that more people will find and appreciate Mac Miller every day. Rest in Peace Mac Miller; The Most Dope.
